Fetal growth week-by-week

WEEK 4

Poppy seed

The embryo is a cluster of cells, starting to form the neural tube.

WEEK 5

Sesame seed

The heart begins to beat; major organs start to develop.

WEEK 6

Sweet pea

The embryo's facial features, arms, and legs are starting to form.

WEEK 7

Blueberry

Limb buds appear, and the brain is developing rapidly.

WEEK 8

Raspberry

Fingers and toes are starting to form; the baby is now called a fetus.

WEEK 9

Olive

The eyes are forming, and the heart has four chambers.

WEEK 10

Prune

All major organs are in place, and the fetus is about the size of a strawberry.

WEEK 11

Lime

The fetus can move, and its fingers and toes are no longer webbed.

WEEK 12

Plum

Reflexes are developing, and the fetus is about the size of a Plum.

WEEK 13

Peach

Bones are starting to harden, and the fetus is growing rapidly.

WEEK 14

Lemon

The baby’s face is becoming more defined, and it can now make facial expressions.

WEEK 15

Apple

The baby can sense light, and hair follicles start to form.

WEEK 16

Avocado

The baby is about the size of an avocado, and limbs are more developed.

WEEK 17

Pear

The baby’s heartbeat can be heard with a stethoscope, and fat is forming.

WEEK 18

Sweet potato

The baby can hear sounds, and the skeleton is hardening.

WEEK 19

Mango

The senses are developing, and the baby may start to move more.

WEEK 20

Banana

The baby is around 10 inches long, halfway through pregnancy.

WEEK 21

Pomegranate

The digestive system is starting to function.

WEEK 22

Papaya

The baby’s eyebrows and hair are becoming visible.

WEEK 23

Grapefruit

The baby’s skin is red and wrinkled, and fingerprints form.

WEEK 24

Cantaloupe

The lungs are developing, preparing for breathing.

WEEK 25

Cauliflower

The baby responds to sound and light and is around 13 inches long.

WEEK 26

Lettuce

The baby’s eyes start to open, and it may react to voices.

WEEK 27

Rutabaga

The baby can practice breathing movements, and the brain is rapidly developing.

WEEK 28

Eggplant

The baby is gaining weight and getting ready for life outside the womb.

WEEK 29

Acorn squah

The baby’s muscles and lungs continue maturing, and it can kick strongly.

WEEK 30

Cucumber

The baby’s skin is becoming smoother, and it is growing fast.

WEEK 31

Pineapple

The baby’s brain is growing, and it may start to gain body fat.

WEEK 32

Squah

The baby’s fingernails and toenails are fully formed.

WEEK 33

Durian

The baby’s bones are fully developed but still soft.

WEEK 34

Butternut squash

The baby’s central nervous system and lungs are almost fully mature.

WEEK 35

Coconut

The baby is gaining weight quickly, and its skin is pink and smooth.

WEEK 36

Honeydew

The baby is practicing breathing and may turn head down for birth.

WEEK 37

Winter melon

The baby is considered full-term and is preparing for delivery.

WEEK 38

Pumpkin

The baby’s organs are fully developed, and it is gaining fat for insulation.

WEEK 39

Watermelon

The baby is ready for birth, with fully developed lungs and reflexes.

WEEK 40

Jackfruit

The baby is full-term and ready to be born.
